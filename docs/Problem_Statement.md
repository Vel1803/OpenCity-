# Problem Statement

## Background
Modern cities depend on multiple government departments — Roads, Water Supply, Drainage, Electricity, Telecom, Gas, and Public Works — to build and maintain public infrastructure.

However, these departments operate independently using separate planning systems, databases, approval processes, and maintenance records. This fragmented approach often results in roads being constructed and then excavated repeatedly by different departments for utility installations or repairs.

## Current Challenges
- Roads are repeatedly excavated after construction.
- Government departments lack coordinated planning.
- Infrastructure data is scattered across departments.
- Public funds are spent multiple times on the same location.
- Traffic congestion increases due to repeated road closures.
- Construction waste and environmental impact increase.
- Citizens have limited visibility into ongoing public projects.
- Asset maintenance is reactive instead of proactive.

## Root Cause
The core issue is the absence of a unified digital platform that enables government departments to:
- Share project schedules.
- View underground utility information.
- Coordinate infrastructure work.
- Maintain a common asset registry.
- Collaborate before project execution.

## Impact

**Government**
- Duplicate expenditure.
- Poor planning.
- Delayed infrastructure projects.
- Higher maintenance costs.

**Citizens**
- Traffic congestion.
- Dust and pollution.
- Delayed public services.
- Reduced trust.

**Environment**
- Increased construction waste.
- Higher carbon emissions.
- Material wastage.
